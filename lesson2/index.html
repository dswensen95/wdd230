Design Principles

Visual Hierarchy

The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

The Church's Website The Church uses visual hierarchy to bring the user’s initial attention to the most important message of the month, the largest “card” at the top-left of the block of “cards”. This month focuses on the theme for General Conference: “Christ is Risen: Faith in Him Will Move Mountains”.

Proximity

The Online Bookclub

The Online Bookclub's Website

The online bookclub’s initial page consists of two columns: one large one first describing what the bookclub is about and then continuing to list books and their official reviews. The second, smaller column lists various “book of the years”. Though the page’s overall design layout contains some flaws that most professional web designers would avoid, this page is a great example of proximity because both columns are grouped together in a way that automatically let’s the user know that each is one object with it’s own objective independent of – though similar to – the other.

White Space

Wikipedia

Wikipedia Website

The startup page of the Wikipedia website is made of so much white space that the user is forced to hone in on the company name and its surrounding links at the center.
